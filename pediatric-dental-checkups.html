Why it matters

Six month visits keep smiles on track

Kids change fast, and so do their teeth. Seeing Dr. Wendy every six months means new teeth, bite changes and early cavities are spotted before they turn into bigger issues. Regular visits also keep the dentist friendly and familiar.

It is one of the simplest, most powerful things a family can do for a child's long term oral health.

Helpful reading: the American Board of Pediatric Dentistry, the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry and CDC oral health guidance.

Building brave, happy habits

A calm, cheerful first experience shapes how a child feels about the dentist for years, so the team takes its time and celebrates every little win.

Checkups work hand in hand with pediatric preventive dentistry, and if a tooth ever needs help, gentle pediatric restorative dentistry is right there.

Good to know

Checkup questions

How often are checkups?

Every six months suits most children, though Dr. Wendy may suggest a different schedule.

When is the first one?

By age one or within six months of the first tooth.

Are X rays taken every time?

No, only when needed, and digital X rays use very low radiation.

What if my child is nervous?

The team is great with nervous kids, using a gentle pace, shows and headphones and lots of encouragement.

How do I prepare my child?

Talk positively, pick a rested time of day and bring a comfort item.
