Special Needs Pediatric Dentistry in San Diego

Sprouting Smiles Pediatric Dentistry has become a name San Diego families share when a child needs extra patience and understanding at the dentist. Every child is welcomed exactly as they are.

A dental home that feels safe

Children with special needs deserve dentistry that meets them where they are, and that is exactly the reputation this practice has earned. Dr. Wendy has years of experience with a wide range of developmental, physical and behavioral needs, and the entire team is trained to offer extra care.

The office can soften the lights and lower the sound to ease sensory overload, turning what could be a stressful trip into a calm, positive one.

Why a specialist

Experience that reassures families

Choosing a dentist who truly understands special needs means a child's individual world is respected from the first hello. Dr. Wendy talks openly with parents about triggers and comforts, so care is personal from day one.

Time and again, families share that their child now feels at ease at the dentist, which is the highest compliment a practice like this can receive.

Helpful reading: the American Board of Pediatric Dentistry, the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry and HealthyChildren.org.

What a first visit looks like

The first appointment is all about building trust. The team learns a child's routines and comforts, invites parents to share what works at home, and mirrors that comfort in the office.

When extra support helps, gentle pediatric sedation dentistry is available, and routine pediatric preventive dentistry keeps visits short and calm.

Ways every child is supported

  • Pre visit planning for comfort
  • A calm, low stimulation room
  • Shows and headphones to distract
  • Extra unhurried time
  • Gentle sedation when helpful
  • Carers welcome to stay close

Good to know

Special needs care questions

Which needs does the team have experience with?

Dr. Wendy cares for kids with many needs, from autism and Down syndrome to cerebral palsy, with a plan for each child.

Can the office lower sensory input?

Yes, the lights can be dimmed and background sound turned off to help sensitive children feel secure.

How do you prepare for a first visit?

Dr. Wendy discusses a child's needs with parents ahead of time so every detail is planned for comfort.

Can a parent stay close?

Always. Carers are welcome to stay so a child feels supported throughout.

Is sedation an option?

Yes, gentle sedation is available for children who need extra help relaxing.
