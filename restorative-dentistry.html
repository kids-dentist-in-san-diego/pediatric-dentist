Why it matters

Baby teeth deserve real care

It is tempting to shrug off a baby tooth, but those little teeth guide the adult ones into place and help kids eat and speak. Left alone, decay can bring pain, infection and crowding later. Timely repairs keep everything on the right track.

Families appreciate that every fix comes with a friendly explanation, so they leave knowing what happened and how to avoid a repeat.

Helpful reading: the American Board of Pediatric Dentistry, ADA MouthHealthy and the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry.

How they keep repairs comfortable

Worried a filling will be a battle? The team goes out of its way to prove otherwise, explaining each step in words kids grasp and offering calming pediatric sedation dentistry when it helps.

And because early action means smaller fixes, steady pediatric preventive dentistry is always the best partner to restorative care.

Signs a repair may be needed

  • A tooth that aches or feels tender
  • Dark spots or visible holes
  • A chip or crack
  • Pain when biting
  • Sensitivity to hot or cold
  • A tooth loosened by a bump

Good to know

Restorative care questions

Why fix a baby tooth?

Baby teeth hold space for adult teeth and help kids eat and speak. Repairing them prevents bigger problems later.

Do fillings hurt?

With gentle techniques and calming options, most kids find fillings easy and are smiling again in no time.

What is a pulpotomy?

A gentle treatment that clears infection from inside a baby tooth so it can be saved.

What is a stainless steel crown?

A sturdy cap that protects a tooth after a big cavity until it falls out naturally.

How do we avoid repairs?

Checkups, fluoride, sealants and solid home habits keep cavities to a minimum.
