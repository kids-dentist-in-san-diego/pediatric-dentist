Good to know

Questions parents often ask

When should a child first see a pediatric dentist?

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry suggests a first visit by age one, or within six months of the first tooth, so a child feels at home early.

How is a pediatric dentist different?

A pediatric dentist trains for years specifically in treating children, from infants to teens, including kids with special needs.

Do they help nervous children?

Yes. The office is built to ease worries, with a friendly team, shows and headphones and calming sedation when needed.

What languages does the team speak?

Families are welcomed in English, Spanish and Mandarin.
