Why it pays off

Small visits now, big smiles later

Because childrens teeth change so quickly, a checkup every six months lets the team catch tiny problems while they are still easy to fix. Families also value that nothing is ever oversold. The advice is honest, the pace is gentle, and the focus stays firmly on keeping kids healthy.

That steady, preventive approach is a big reason the practice has earned such a loyal following across the Sabre Springs area.

Simple routines that keep cavities away

Good habits at home do most of the heavy lifting between visits, and the team is generous with practical, easy to follow tips. A few small changes add up to a real difference over the years.

Strong prevention also pairs naturally with their pediatric restorative dentistry and routine pediatric dental checkups, so nothing gets missed.

Handy tips for parents

  • Brush morning and night
  • Use a tiny smear of fluoride paste for toddlers
  • Supervise brushing until about age eight
  • Trade sugary drinks for water
  • Turn flossing into a bedtime game
  • Keep those six month visits

Good to know

Preventive care questions

How often should kids visit for prevention?

A cleaning and exam every six months suits most children, and Dr. Wendy adjusts that when a child needs it.

Are sealants worth it?

Absolutely. This quick, painless coating shields the grooves of back teeth and is one of the best cavity blockers around.

Is fluoride necessary?

Fluoride hardens enamel and fights decay. The team offers gentle in office treatments as part of preventive care.

When is the first visit?

Experts suggest by age one or within six months of the first tooth.

What happens during a checkup?

A cleaning, a friendly exam, fluoride when useful and simple home care tips.
